Studio notes

A tiny research studio that treats content in Austin like a science project.

I walk, run, sit, and talk my way through Austin and turn what I see into content systems for founders and brands who want something a little more human than another funnel screenshot.

Illustration or weird Austin drawing lives here. For now it is imagination only.

No big agency. No massive team. Just one person taking notes in this city and building formats that actually match the people who live here.

Portfolio

Some experiments on the Austin timeline

Most of my work looks simple on the surface. Underneath is a mix of interviews, field notes, and quiet observation about what people stop for, reply to, and share with a friend.

Street interviews

Tiny Friendships on the sidewalk

A weekly experiment where I stop strangers in Austin and ask about friendship, careers, and how they actually build a life here. Short clips, longer cuts, real people.

Format: short form plus YouTube. Focus: real community, not fake hype.
Run and talk

Coffee after the run

I join people on their run, ask uncomfortable questions at a conversational pace, then finish with coffee and a quick interview. Part therapy, part research about what matters.

Format: 3 to 6 minute cuts. Location: Austin trails and coffee shops.
Content audit

Austin feed research file

A slow file of screenshots, notes, and patterns about what works on Austin social feeds. Saved posts, common hooks, phrases that are overused, and the rare ones that still feel alive.

Output: pattern library and content ideas for clients.
Show design

The founder with a neighbor energy

A show format for local founders who want to sound like a neighbor, not a press release. Structure, questions, and segment ideas included, tailored to their Austin niche.

Deliverable: show skeleton, pilot script, episode map.
